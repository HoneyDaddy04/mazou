**Version 1.0 | March 2026**

## The Story

### The Naira Problem

In 2023, the naira collapsed. AWS bills tripled overnight. Every African startup running cloud infrastructure watched their runway shrink  - not because they built badly, but because the currency moved.

AI API costs are following the same path.

Today, a Nigerian developer building a customer support bot needs an OpenAI account (USD), an Anthropic account (USD), maybe a Google account (USD). Three API keys. Three dashboards. Three invoices in a currency that isn't theirs. And when the finance team asks "what are we spending on AI?"  - nobody can answer with precision.

This isn't a minor inconvenience. It's a structural barrier to building with AI in Africa.

### The Gap

Global AI gateways exist. OpenRouter aggregates models. AWS Bedrock offers multi-model access. But none of them:
- Accept naira (or any African currency)
- Include African language models
- Integrate with Paystack or local payment rails
- Understand that a Naira-denominated prepaid wallet isn't a nice-to-have  - it's a requirement

They weren't built for this market. Not because they don't care, but because they don't need to.

Someone does.

### The Fix

Mazou is one API key that connects to 40+ AI models  - GPT, Claude, Gemini, Llama, DeepSeek, and African-built models for Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo, Swahili.

You fund a wallet in naira through Paystack. You make API calls. Our smart routing picks the best model for each request  - optimizing for cost, quality, and language.

Your dashboard shows exactly what every feature costs. Not "total API spend last month." But: "Your support bot used GPT-4o for 842K calls and spent 1.9M naira  - switching to Claude Sonnet would save you 890K."

That's the product. Simple to explain. Hard to build. Impossible to ignore once you've seen the dashboard.

### The Ambition

Paystack built the payment rails for Africa's internet economy. Before Paystack, accepting payments online in Nigeria was a nightmare of bank integrations, failed transactions, and regulatory puzzles. Paystack made it one line of code.

Mazou is doing the same for AI.

Not because the analogy sounds good in a pitch deck. Because the pattern is the same: a fragmented, dollar-denominated, opaque infrastructure problem that requires someone who understands the local context to solve properly.

The developers are here. 500,000+ in Nigeria alone, growing 50%+ annually. The AI models are here. The demand is here. The infrastructure to connect them  - affordably, visibly, in local currency  - wasn't.

Now it is.

## Narrative Principles

### 1. Problem Before Product
Always lead with the problem. The product matters because the problem matters. Never open with "Mazou is..."  - open with what's broken.

### 2. Specific Over General
"842K calls, 1.9M naira, switch to Sonnet, save 890K" is more compelling than "we help you save money on AI." Numbers create belief. Specificity creates trust.

### 3. Show the Dashboard
The dashboard is the proof. Every pitch, every landing page, every demo should show real (or realistic) numbers in a real interface. The product sells itself when people see their potential savings.

### 4. The Paystack Parallel Is Earned
Use the Paystack comparison deliberately. It's powerful because it's accurate  - same market, same pattern, same structural problem. But don't overuse it. Mazou needs to stand on its own.

### 5. African by Context, Not Costume
The brand is African because the problems it solves are African. Local currency billing, African language routing, Paystack integration  - these are the proof points. Not colors, not patterns, not stock photos of Lagos skylines.

### 6. Infrastructure Is Quiet
Infrastructure doesn't need to be loud. Paystack's brand isn't flashy  - it's trustworthy. Mazou should feel the same way: solid, clear, precise. The product is confident enough to speak plainly.

## Brand Story Arc (For Presentations)

### Act 1: The World Is Broken (30%)
- AI costs are the new cloud costs
- Dollar-denominated, invisible, growing fast
- African developers are taxed twice: once for the API, once for the FX
- No one has the full picture of what they're spending

### Act 2: Why It's Still Broken (20%)
- Global solutions don't work here
- OpenRouter is USD-only, no African models
- AWS Bedrock is enterprise-only, complex
- Local currency billing + African language routing = unsolved

### Act 3: The Fix (30%)
- One API, 40+ models, naira billing
- Smart routing saves 15-40%
- Dashboard shows per-feature costs
- Two modes: managed or BYOK
- OpenAI-compatible = zero friction

### Act 4: The Vision (20%)
- Paystack parallel: infrastructure for Africa's AI economy
- Nigeria first, then Ghana and Kenya
- Network effects: more usage = better routing = lower costs
- AI agents will need wallets, commerce, identity  - Mazou is the layer
