## The Problem We Solve

African developers building with AI face a structural problem:

1. **Fragmented access**  - 10 API keys, 10 dashboards, 10 billing accounts to use leading AI models
2. **Dollar-only billing**  - Getting a USD virtual card and managing FX is a tax on building with AI in Africa
3. **Zero cost visibility**  - No way to know which feature, agent, or model is driving spend
4. **Invisible African models**  - Local language models exist but are scattered and hard to access

## How Mazou Works

### Two Access Models

**Managed Mode**  - Mazou holds the provider API keys. Developers fund a naira wallet through Paystack and access all models immediately. Best for startups and SMEs who want zero setup.

**BYOK (Bring Your Own Keys)**  - Developers plug in their existing API keys. Mazou adds the smart routing, cost tracking, and optimization layer on top. Best for enterprises with existing provider contracts.

### Smart Routing
Mazou's routing engine automatically selects the optimal model for each API request based on:
- Budget constraints
- Language requirements (especially African languages)
- Task complexity
- Model performance characteristics

### Cost Visibility Dashboard
Per-feature, per-model spend attribution with actionable optimization recommendations. Teams see exactly what every AI feature costs and get specific recommendations to reduce spend.

## FAQ for Press

**Q: How is Mazou different from OpenRouter?**
A: OpenRouter is a multi-model gateway built for the global (primarily US) market. It's USD-only, doesn't support African language models, and doesn't offer per-feature cost attribution. Mazou is built specifically for Africa  - local currency billing through Paystack, African language model routing, and cost visibility designed for teams managing AI spend in volatile currency environments.

**Q: Who are your target customers?**
A: African startups and SMEs building AI features  - fintech, healthtech, edtech, agritech. Initially focused on Nigeria, expanding to Ghana and Kenya.

**Q: What's the business model?**
A: In managed mode, we add a 15% margin on top of provider costs. In BYOK mode, we charge a platform fee for routing, analytics, and optimization.

**Q: Are you live?**
A: The platform is built and tested with 30 production endpoints and 200+ automated tests. We're preparing for beta launch in Nigeria.

**Q: How much have you raised?**
A: We're raising a $500K pre-seed round to fund Nigeria launch, team growth, and expansion to Ghana and Kenya.
